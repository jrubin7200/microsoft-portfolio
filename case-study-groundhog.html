IP Adaptation · Sony / Tequila Works (2018-2019)

Groundhog Day: Like Father Like Son

Finding the soul of a beloved IP and rebuilding it for a new medium.

The Challenge

What is the Soul of Groundhog Day?

I pitched the game to Sony and secured one of the most beloved comedy IPs of all time. Then I secured funding from investors, worked with our team to select a development studio, and signed on as Narrative Director — overseeing everything from the initial narrative design to hiring and leading the writing team, collaborating with the film's original screenwriter, directing voice actors, and guiding implementation.

But the real challenge wasn't logistics. It was this: What is the soul of Groundhog Day?

A time loop is just a mechanic. The movie endures because it's about something deeper. If we got that wrong, we'd have a gimmick, not an experience worth the IP.

The Approach

Spelunking for the Soul

Finding the soul of an IP requires spelunking deep into the film's architecture — reading everything about it, talking to the original film's screenwriter, studying the film's structure. And then — just meditating quietly with the feelings left inside. Letting the emotional core surface.

What emerged became a brand guide that defined the pillars of the IP:

The Tragedy of Groundhog Day

Phil's trapped on the WORST day of his life. It isn't just any random day. It's a day when the worst possible version of every situation happens — when his greatest desires are thwarted and his worst traits are exposed.

The Fantasy of Groundhog Day

Achieving the perfect day. We all dream of going back to fix what we got wrong. To kiss the girl. To punch the bully. To come up with a better punchline. The core fantasy is that by reliving our worst day, we can finally get everything right.

The Surprise

It's not about repetition — it's about variation. Despite waking to the same day every day, there is always more to discover within a moment than you could possibly take in the first time.

The Journey

From selfishness to selflessness. Phil thinks his goal is to escape the loop. But that's only what he wants, not what he needs. He finally escapes when he learns to care more about others than himself.

What I Learned

The Emotional Engine

Adapting a beloved IP isn't about recreating its plot. It's about identifying the emotional engine underneath, the film's soul — then rebuilding it for a new medium.

A trustworthy AI companion also needs more than good mechanics and design. It needs a soul: a consistent emotional core that users can feel, even if they can't articulate it. Defining that — what it values, how it grows, what journey it's on with you — is the same work I did for Groundhog Day.
